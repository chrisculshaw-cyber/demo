Swarm help

Found a swarm? Act quickly — and skip the email.

Swarms need attending to fast, and an inbox is the slowest route to a beekeeper. Use the swarm collector map to phone the nearest volunteer directly.

  • We only deal with  honey bees  — not wasps, hornets or bumblebees.
  • On private land, the landowner's permission is needed before anything can be done.
  • Collection is usually free — a settled cluster is a healthy colony looking for a home.
Find a swarm collector

Not sure what you're looking at?

A honey bee swarm is a football-sized cluster of thousands of bees, usually hanging from a branch, fence or wall. It appears suddenly, hums loudly, and is surprisingly docile — the bees are full of honey and have nothing to defend.

Single insects going in and out of a hole in the ground or a roof are more likely wasps or bumblebees — a swarm collector can't help with those, but a local pest controller can advise.

Bee health

Yellow-legged (Asian) hornet Report sightings

This invasive hornet preys on honey bees and is now being found in the UK each year. If you think you've seen one, photograph it if safe and report it through the Asian Hornet Watch app or online — every confirmed sighting matters. Also see our guidance on poisoning, spray liaison for beekeepers and farmers, and general colony health.
